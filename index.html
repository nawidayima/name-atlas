A name that traveled

Sanskrit at its root, Buddhist in its journey, at home in an American classroom. This page holds our criteria and twenty-seven candidates in plain alphabetical order, each with its etymology, its journey, and links to the original sources. The choosing is ours.

The word "Zen" is half of a Chinese attempt to spell a Sanskrit sound, and the meaning rode inside the pronunciation across four languages. The same current carried names south into Thailand, Burma, Cambodia and Java. Names can hold both our worlds. Some already do.

Sanskrit · via Thailand

Arun अरुण

ah-ROON · "dawn"

From aruṇa, the red glow before sunrise, personified as the charioteer who drives the sun's horses into the sky. The figure crossed into Southeast Asia with Buddhism: Bangkok's riverside Temple of Dawn, Wat Arun, carries the same name.

common in India & Thailand · rare in the US · already short

watch: reads male across South and Southeast Asia

Sanskrit

Aruni अरुणि

ah-ROO-nee · "of the dawn"

A patronymic of aruṇa, carried by Uddālaka Āruṇi of the Chāndogya Upaniṣad, who taught his son with a demonstration: salt dissolved overnight in water, invisible yet present in every sip. The Indologist Walter Ruben counted him among the earliest natural philosophers on record.

no recorded US births · unisex in India & Nepal, F in Sri Lanka · short: Aru, Runi

watch: a permanent spell-it-out name

Sanskrit · via Java

Bayu

BAH-yoo · "wind"

The Javanese and Indonesian form of vāyu, the Vedic god of wind and breath. In the Javanese shadow-play tradition Bayu is the divine father of Hanuman and of the hero Bima, and the name is an everyday Indonesian given name today.

common in Indonesia, M-leaning there · rare in the US · already short

watch: English readers may say BAY-you

Sanskrit

Bodhi बोधि

BOH-dee · "awakening"

From the root budh, to wake: bodhi is the awakening itself, and the fig tree at Bodh Gaya under which it happened is the bodhi tree.

US #265 boys · Australia top 100 · M-leaning · already short

watch: the popularity is the caveat

Sanskrit

Indra इन्द्र

IN-drah · king of the Vedic gods

Indra wields the thunderbolt and commands the storm. Buddhism kept him and hung a metaphor on his palace: Indra's net, a lattice of jewels in which every jewel reflects all the others, the Huayan school's image of interdependence and a favorite of writers connecting Buddhism and physics.

~25 US births, split M/F · Indra Nooyi as professional precedent

watch: gender expectations differ by region; Indira confusion

Sanskrit · everywhere south and east

Jaya जय

JAY-ah or JAH-yah · "victory"

From the root ji, to win. Few words traveled further: Jakarta began as Jayakarta, "complete victory," and the Buddhist king Jayavarman VII, "protected by jaya," built Angkor's Bayon. Feminine in India, masculine across much of Southeast Asia, so it lands neutral.

not in US top 1000 · genuinely unisex across regions · already short

watch: two natural pronunciations; pick one and coach it

Hawaiian / Japanese / Scandinavian

Kai

KY · "sea" (Hawaiian)

Not Sanskrit: Hawaiian for sea, a Japanese given name, a Scandinavian short form. On this page because it defines the sound the shortlist competes with.

US #93 boys · Victoria #32 · M-leaning · already short

watch: several per classroom by 2030

Sanskrit

Kavi कवि

KUH-vee · "seer-poet"

In the Ṛgveda a kavi is a seer before he is a poet: the sage who perceives ṛta, the hidden order of the cosmos, and puts it into words. The closest Sanskrit comes to one word for looking at the world and seeing what is actually there.

66 US births 2024, rising · 92% M in use; American Girl's 2023 Girl of the Year is Kavi Sharma · already short

watch: cavity jokes; KAH/KAY vowel coaching

Sanskrit / Japanese / Turkish / Hopi / Noongar

Kaya काय

KY-ah

In Buddhist philosophy kāya is "body," as in dharmakāya, the truth-body of a buddha. The same sound is separately a Japanese tree name, Turkish for rock, Hopi for elder sister, and a Noongar greeting in southwest Australia.

268 US girls 2024, drifting down from a 2005 peak · F-leaning

watch: Kaia is the spelling currently in fashion

Sanskrit · via all of Buddhist Asia

Meru मेरु

MAY-roo · the world-mountain

The axial mountain of Buddhist cosmology, usually Sumeru, "wondrous Meru," around which the sun, moon and stars were said to circle. It crossed into Japanese as Shumisen and Korean as Sumisan.

no recorded US births · no established gender anywhere · already short

watch: rhymes with Peru; expect "did you invent it?"

Sanskrit · via Korea & Japan · also Slavic/Finnish

Miro 미륵 · 弥勒

MEE-roh · from "loving-kindness"

Sanskrit mitra (friend) grew into maitrī (loving-kindness) and then Maitreya, the future Buddha, who crossed China into Korean Mireuk and Japanese Miroku. Korea's seventh-century Gilt-bronze Pensive Bodhisattva is Mireuk. Unrelated but real: Miro is a common Finnish name and a Slavic short form of mir, "peace, world."

~23 US births · M-leaning · already the short form

watch: the Miro whiteboard app; Milo mix-ups

Sanskrit · traveled west too

Mitra मित्र

MEE-trah · "friend, covenant"

From an old Indo-Iranian word for "that which binds": Mitra is the god of pacts and friendship, first written down in a treaty around 1400 BCE. He went west as Persian Mithra and Roman Mithras while his root traveled east inside Maitreya. A common feminine name in Iran today.

10 US births 2024, all girls

watch: reads as a Bengali surname to many Indians

Sanskrit

Nachiketa नचिकेत

nuh-chi-KAY-tuh

The boy of the Kaṭha Upaniṣad who waited three days at Death's door, was offered wealth, herds and long life, and refused everything except the answer to what happens after death. Yama, Death himself, finally taught him.

no recorded US births · M in India · short: Keta

watch: the natural short form Nachi invites nacho jokes and worse mishearings

Sanskrit · the Japan link is coincidence

Nara नर

NAH-rah · "human being"

Sanskrit nara is the human being, and Nārāyaṇa, "he who rests on the waters," is Vishnu on the cosmic ocean. The Japanese city of Nara, for all its Buddhist history, most likely takes its name from a native word for flattened land; the match is homophony, and Korean nara, "country," is likewise its own word.

72 US girls 2024, doubling year on year · F in US use

watch: the influencer Nara Smith is spiking it; recheck the May 2027 data

Sanskrit · via Tibet

Pema པདྨ

PEH-mah · "lotus"

The Tibetan pronunciation of padma, the lotus: the flower that roots in mud and opens clean, Buddhism's oldest image of awakening. Pema Chödrön gave the name a familiar American face.

10 US births 2024, all girls · already short

watch: the first syllable is easy fuel for six-year-olds

Sanskrit

Reva रेवा

RAY-vah · "she who leaps"

An epithet of the Narmadā, one of India's seven sacred rivers, from a root meaning to leap or bound. A river name with a modern sound, next door to Nova and Ava.

63 US girls 2024, flat for a decade · F-leaning · already short

watch: little; a minor Star Wars character shares it

Sanskrit

Saryu सरयू

SUH-ryoo · a river of the Ṛgveda

The river of Ayodhya, named in the Ṛgveda as Sarayu, from the root sṛ, to flow. A quiet geography note from one side of the family.

essentially unused in the US · F-leaning in India

watch: stress varies in English mouths; Sarayu is the more self-pronouncing spelling

Sanskrit / Korean / Japanese / Turkish

Sena सेना

SAY-nah

Sanskrit senā is "army." The same sound lives independently as a Korean girls' name, a Japanese given name, and a Turkish name from Arabic thanāʾ, "praise." Ayrton Senna is the glamorous near miss.

60 US births 2024, mostly girls · already short

watch: John Cena memes; cena is "dinner" in Spanish

Sanskrit · via Japan

Setsuna 刹那

seh-tsoo-nah · "an instant"

Sanskrit kṣaṇa, the Abhidharma's smallest unit of time, roughly a seventy-fifth of a second, was spelled into Chinese as 刹那 and read in Japanese as setsuna. The teaching attached to it: live fully even inside the briefest flicker.

rare everywhere · anime-coded in Japan · short: Setsu

watch: one sound from setsunai, "heartache"

Sanskrit · Greek homograph

Soma सोम

SOH-mah · the moon-elixir

The pressed elixir and moon of the Ṛgveda; the entire ninth book is soma hymns. Unrelated Greek sōma, "body," gives biology the soma of a neuron, and Sōma is separately a current Japanese boys' name.

~19 US births 2024, split M/F · already short

watch: Brave New World's drug is assigned reading, and Soma is a prescription muscle relaxant

Japanese / Korean · the Sumeru echo is coincidence

Sumi

SOO-mee

Japanese Sumi is written with characters for clarity, or for ink (sumi-e is ink-wash painting); Korean Sumi is its own name again. The echo of Sumeru's first syllable is a pun, not a lineage.

near zero US births · F-leaning · already short

watch: "sue me"

Sanskrit · via Tibet · also Irish

Tara तारा

TAH-rah · "star; she who carries across"

From tṝ, to cross: tārā is the star that guides and the saviouress who ferries beings over the ocean of saṃsāra. In legend she was born from a tear of Avalokiteśvara. The Irish royal Hill of Tara is unrelated but adds a western foothold.

247 US girls 2024, just out of the top 1000 after decades inside · F

watch: reads as a parent-generation name in the US and Australia

Sanskrit · via Bali

Tirta

TEER-tah · "sacred crossing"

Sanskrit tīrtha is a ford: the shallow place where you can cross a river, and by extension every pilgrimage site where the ordinary crosses toward the sacred. In Bali tirta is holy water itself, as at the spring temple Tirta Empul.

used in Indonesia · rare in the US · already short

watch: English speakers may flatten it to TUR-ta

Sanskrit

Veda वेद

VAY-dah · "knowledge"

From vid, to know, cognate with Latin videre and English wit: veda is knowledge itself, and the name of the oldest Indian scriptures.

US #692 girls, climbing for twenty years · F in US use · already short

watch: already discovered; carries scriptural weight

Sanskrit

Yukti युक्ति

YOOK-tee · "reasoning"

From yuj, to yoke, the same root as yoga: yukti is reasoning as the yoking-together of ideas, a technical term for logical method in Indian philosophy and Ayurveda.

F in India · rare in the US · short: Yuki

watch: English mouths drift to "YUCK-tee"

Sanskrit · via China, Korea & Japan

Zen 禅

ZEN · from "meditative absorption"

Sanskrit dhyāna became Pali jhāna, was spelled into Chinese as 禪那 chánnà and clipped to chán, crossed to Korea as Sŏn and to Japan as Zen. The name is one syllable of a 1,500-year journey.

277 US births 2024, near the top-1000 doorstep · M-leaning · already short

watch: reads as a word name; "so zen" jokes

Sanskrit · via Burma

Zeya ဇေယျ

ZAY-yah · "victory"

The Burmese form of jaya through Pali jeyya, a standard element of Burmese royal and personal names for centuries. The same victory that named Jakarta, wearing a Burmese sound.

rare in the US · unisex-leaning sound · already short

watch: stress varies; ZAY-ah vs zeh-YAH

One last proof that words travel: Canon cameras are named for Kannon, the Japanese form of Avalokiteśvara. Compassion itself, arrived in Japan and then in your pocket. A good name goes far.
